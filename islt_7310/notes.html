The staff is what we use to both read and write music. The staff consists of five lines and a clef based on the instrument we are using (circled). For this demonstration we will be using the Treble Clef, the most common
- There are seven natural notes in the musical language (A>F). They are notated by their position in the staff. The height of the notes represents their register - notes that are toward the bottom sound lower, and notes that are toward the top sound higher.
- Notes on the lines can be memorized by the acrostic: 'Every Good Boy Deserves Fudge'. Notes in the spaces spell 'FACE'. If you were to line up the notes in order, we would have E, F, G, A, B, C, D, E.
