Accidentals are characters placed in front of notes to either raise or lower them. In truth, there are 12 total notes in the musical language.
- A sharp (#) raises the note between itself and the next note.
- A flat (b) lowers the note between itself and the next note
> In between notes can have two names. For instance, the note between a C and a D can be called both C# and Db. You typically will notate it based on if you are traveling up (C#) or down (Db) the staff.
