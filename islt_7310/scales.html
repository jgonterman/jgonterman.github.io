An octave is the distance between one note (like C) and the next note bearing its same name. It is the same note, just an octave higher or lower.

A scale is 8 notes, from the first note to its octave. There are many different scales, but we will use the Major Scale for this example. The Major Scale is made up of the pattern - Whole, Whole, Half, Whole, Whole, Whole, Half.
- A Half Step is the next note in order. A Whole Step is the second note in order.
> For example, in the Eb Major Scale, we start with the note Eb. Then we skip the next note (E), and the second note for our scale becomes F. Then we skip the next note (F#), and the third note of our scale becomes G. Because the next step in the Major Scale is only a Half Step, we use the next note (Ab) as the fourth note in our scale.
